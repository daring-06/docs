Feedback is one of the highest-leverage ways you can contribute to openpilot as a user. Whether you notice something off about how your car drives, think driver monitoring is being too aggressive, or run into a software bug, there is a clear path for getting that information to the team.

## Driving Feedback

If you have feedback about how your car drives on openpilot, join the community Discord and submit it through the dedicated channel.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Join the community Discord">
    Head to [discord.comma.ai](https://discord.comma.ai) and join the server if you haven't already.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open #submit-feedback">
    Navigate to the `#submit-feedback` channel and use the form there to post your driving feedback.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Before posting, confirm all three prerequisites below are met. Feedback submitted without meeting these conditions is much harder for the team to act on.
</Note>

<Accordion title="Driving feedback prerequisites">
- **openpilot is up to date** — you should be on the latest openpilot release or nightly build.
- **Both road-facing cameras have a clear view** — your windshield and camera lenses should be clean and unobstructed.
- **Your device is mounted properly** — mount your device horizontally centered and relatively high on the windshield.
</Accordion>

## Driver Monitoring Feedback

If driver monitoring (DM) is flagging you while you are being perfectly attentive, those are likely false positives that the team wants to fix. Driver monitoring feedback is highly actionable — the team can often address your specific complaint within a single release cycle.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Join the community Discord">
    Go to [discord.comma.ai](https://discord.comma.ai) and join the server.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable driver camera recording">
    If driver camera recording is toggled off in your device settings, temporarily turn it on and drive until you reproduce the issue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the relevant segment in comma connect">
    Open [comma connect](https://connect.comma.ai) and locate the route where the false positive occurred. Identify the specific segment — routes are split into one-minute chunks — then upload that segment's logs and driver camera footage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post in #openpilot-experience">
    Share the uploaded segment link in the `#openpilot-experience` channel on Discord. Include a clear description of what happened and why you believe it was a false positive.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
You can turn driver camera recording back off after you have reproduced and uploaded the issue. Leaving it enabled indefinitely is not required.
</Warning>

<Accordion title="Driver monitoring feedback prerequisites">
- **openpilot is up to date** — you should be on the latest openpilot release or nightly build.
- **The driver camera has a clear view of the driver** — make sure nothing blocks the camera (e.g. a cable), and that the lens is clean.
- **Your device is mounted properly** — mount your device horizontally centered and relatively high on the windshield.
</Accordion>

## Other Bugs

Got an issue that doesn't fit into driving or driver monitoring feedback? Open an issue directly on the openpilot GitHub repository.

<Card title="Open a GitHub Issue" icon="github" href="https://github.com/commaai/openpilot/issues/new/choose">
  Use the issue tracker to report bugs, unexpected behavior, or anything else that doesn't belong in Discord.
</Card>
